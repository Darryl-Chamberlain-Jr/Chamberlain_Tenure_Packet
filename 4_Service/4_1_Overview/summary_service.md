# Overview
I have active leadership experience at all levels of ERAU-W as well as in my professional community. I will summarize my most impactful service first, then describe all of my service in the past five years while at ERAU-W. 

Upon starting at ERAU-W, I joined the College of Arts & Science's (COAS) Faculty Council as well as chaired the ERAU-W Faculty Senate Academic Technology committee. These service opportunities allowed me to begin building collegal relationships in other departments in COAS as well as colleagues in College of Aeronautics (COA) and College of Business (COB). After the sudden passing of Dr. Karen Keene in February 2023, I stepped up to serve as Associate Chair for the department of Mathematics, Science, and Technology. After a failed department chair hiring search and Beverly Wood stepping down from temporary chair duties, I was both Acting Department Chair and Associate Department Chair starting January 2024. After the successful chair search yielded Timothy Smith as the new Department Chair starting August 2024, I returned to just Assoicate Department Chair and supported his transition from ERAU-DB to ERAU-W. In August 2025 I transitioned from Associate Department Chair as I became Program Chair for the newly established Bachelor in Data Science and needed to teach many of the specialty courses for the program. 

Outside of Embry-Riddle, I am involved in the Mathematical Association of America (MAA), the Special Interest Group on Research in Undergraduate Mathematics Education (RUME), and the Special Interest Group on Statistics and Data Science Education (SDS-Ed). The MAA is the largest national association of American and Canadian mathematicians with a membership of over 24,000. RUME was the first Special Interest Group of the MAA and is the largest national association of collegiate mathematics education researchers in the United States. SDS-Ed, originally focused solely on statistics education, is a one of the largest national associations for Data Science education in the United States. I serve as a member on the MAA Council on Teaching and Learning, a member on the MAA Committee on Teaching Undergraduate Mathematics (CTUM), and as the chair for the Subcommittee on Technology in Mathematics Education (STME). These groups allow for interaction and influence of college educators, especially with relation to technology. I have served small committees in RUME as a stepping stone toward running for a leadership role within the research community, though my recent change to leading the Bachelor in Data Science and shift to Educational Data Science research has led to more interaction in SDS-Ed.
